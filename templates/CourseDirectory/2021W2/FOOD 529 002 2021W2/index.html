COURSE DESCRIPTION:

This course (lecture & lab) is designed to equip students with the skills to conduct objective (scientifically sound) assessment of foods. It will provide students with the necessary expertise to conduct scientifically sound, statistically valid evaluations for the food and beverages industries. Students will get hands-on experience with the data collection, data analysis and interpretation of experimental results during eight laboratory sessions. Emphasis is placed on experiment design and the application and use of statistics. Students will improve their oral and written communication skills. Students will prepare executive summaries. This course is intended for students interested in sensory evaluation of food and beverages. However, it is beneficial to all students desiring hands-on experience with statistical analyses and interpretation of experimental data.

COURSE OBJECTIVES:

    1. to develop an understanding of sensory psychology and physiology
    2. to select and implement appropriate sensory methodology for a specified objective
    3. to understand the capabilities and limitations of sensory tests
    4. to gain experience in data collection and analysis and interpretation of sensory data
    5. to gain hands-on experience with conducting statistical analyses
    6. to develop written and oral communication skills
    7. to gain experience with the preparation of executive summaries, describing: ‘what was done’; ‘how was it  done’; ‘what was found’; and ‘what was concluded’.

ASSESSMENT OF LEARNING

The assessment of learning will be based on six written laboratory reports (LABS 1, 2, 3, 5, 6 and 8), one oral presentation (Lab 4), and two executive summaries (Labs 4 and 7). See table above.

The laboratory reports are NOT formal reports, but rather a series of 'answers' to questions, as listed in the lab manual. However, students are expected to cite references in their reports. The questions for the report are available in advance, in CANVAS, and in the LABORATORY MANUAL (Cliff, 2022). Students may work with a partner to conduct the statistical analyzes (EXCEL, MINITAB) but must prepare the written reports themselves.

The oral presentation (LAB 4) is also ‘answer' to questions, as listed in the lab manual. The questions are available in advance, in CANVAS and in the LABORATORY MANUAL (Cliff, 2022). Students will work with a partner for their oral presentation. 

Should it be necessary to revise the assessment plan, any changes will be discussed with the students. A revised syllabus would be dated, emailed to students, and posted in CANVAS.

ADDITIONAL LABORATORY INFORMATION

The laboratory experiments are teaching tools. They are designed to introduce and demonstrate sensory principles. Since students will participate as experimenters and judges, students may be more informed than usual. The lab objectives are NOT to test the students’ tasting (smelling) abilities, but rather to provide experimental data for statistical analyses and interpretation.

After each laboratory, students will decode and tabulate the classes’ responses using an Excel spreadsheet. Data will be posted for statistical analyses and report preparation.

SPECIAL GROOMING

Students are instructed not to apply scented toiletries (perfumes, after-shaves, hand-creams) on the day of the laboratory. Lab coats will be provided for in-person participation in the sensory laboratory. If laboratories occur via on-line instruction, students are encouraged to evaluate the samples from their take-home laboratory kits in a well-ventilated area that is free of distractions.
