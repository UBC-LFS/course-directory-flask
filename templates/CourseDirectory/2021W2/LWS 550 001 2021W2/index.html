Strong communication skills and the ability to work cooperatively in a team are qualities that are consistently in demand by employers. It is particularly important to effectively communicate and collaborate on issues related to land and water, as they are inherently complex and involve multiple jurisdictions and stakeholders. We also live in a time where we have access to many tools that can enhance our communications by making them more engaging through online platforms and digital media. These tools can help to connect the science of land and water systems to non-scientists, such as planners, policy makers and members of the community.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, learners will:

Gain the skills required, through the application of critical thinking and assessment, to prepare and deliver professional communications of complex information in a collaborative team environment effectively and credibly, by employing digital technologies congruent with audience needs.

More specifically, students should be able to:

  1. Identify a target audience and assess their information needs.
  2. Describe different communication formats/delivery used by professionals and their purpose.
  3. Evaluate professional communications by applying critical thinking and analysis to published communications and information.
  4. Discuss ethical considerations of professionals and their communications.
  5. Develop and deliver professional communications in a credible, academically rigorous and effective manner.
  6. Work collaboratively in a team environment.
  7. Develop competence in the (professional) use of digital technologies (e.g., data visualization, website development, emerging technologies).

Evaluation of Course Participation

Participation is worth 20% of the LWS 550 course. Students are expected to attend all classes and actively engage in discussions and other participatory activities (including Professional Ethics Case Studies, Environmental Round Table, Peer Evaluation of Student Writing, Hands-on Digital Media Tutorials, Team Project Activities, including developing a Team Charter, Communication Action Plan and Final Deliverable), both in-person and online.

There are a variety of different modes in which students can contribute to the course learning environment and earn participation marks, designed for different preferences. These include synchronous verbal discussions (in-person and online), role-playing activity, synchronous Q & A during lectures (both verbal and via chat), asynchronous written discussion questions and reflections on Canvas, group work, and peer evaluation (both in-person and in writing).

Course participation will be evaluated by the instructor, with marks and comments tabulated for each student throughout the duration of the course using the following evaluation rubric as a guide. A cumulative participation mark (worth 20%) will be recorded at the end of the course.

  • A score of "1" would be awarded if the student had poor class attendance, failed to prepare for the class, had not listened carefully to what other classmates were saying, had either not communicated or communicated poorly during the class, had been disrespectful and/or had made no contribution in class.
  • A score of "2" would be awarded if the student’s awareness, listening skills, communication, respectfulness and contributions allowed the student to make small but limited contributions to the class discussion.
  • A score of "3" would be awarded if the student’s awareness, listening skills, communication, respectfulness and contributions allowed that student to participate actively and courteously in the class discussion and enhance the class’s understanding of the subject materials, the issues at hand and provide some suggestions for resolving the issues under discussion.
  • A score of "4" would be awarded if the student’s awareness was very good, listened carefully at all times to other classmates, communicated clearly, respected the contributions of other members and made a substantial contribution to the understanding and resolution of the issues in discussion.
  • A score of "5" would be awarded if the student demonstrated outstanding and/or excellent performance in any category. They consistently took a proactive, leadership position in activities and actively listened to and facilitated communications with other students to create a collaborative, enhanced learning environment.

Note: This rubric provides a guide for participation evaluation and is subject to change as the course offering may adapt throughout the term to different circumstances.
