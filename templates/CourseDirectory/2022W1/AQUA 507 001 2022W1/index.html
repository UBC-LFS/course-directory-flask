Academic Calendar Entry

Seafood Processing focuses on novel and traditional food processing methods and technologies used in B.C., and Canada. We will study CFIA regulations and GFSI food safety and quality management systems to attain aquaculture products' quality and safety compliance. The sustainability and environmental impacts of Seafood Processing are also reviewed.

 

Course Format and Class Dynamic

This course will be taught in the classroom. Every week chosen readings are assigned for students to read and study.

 

Students will apply concepts and knowledge attained in the readings and in the classroom to identify food safety and food quality control points for specific seafood processes.

 

Real seafood process case scenarios would be reviewed to evaluate product safety and product quality, allowing students to assess and define process controls, monitoring activities, verification procedures, corrective actions (root cause analysis), and record-keeping to maintain and properly manage safety and quality plans, that are both regulatory and customer specifications compliant.

 

The course starts from a general view of the seafood industry in Canada, seafood chemistry followed by principles of seafood processing, and into the fundamentals for process controls for seafood products safety and quality compliance. The last third of the syllabus will cover specific seafood products quality and safety issues.

 

On week 12 we will have a field trip to a seafood processing facility and for our last class, we expect a visit of a guest lecturer from CFIA to talk about regulatory compliance and licensing of seafood processing facilities.

 

Each week in this tentative syllabus represents a 2-hour class.

 

 

Syllabus

 

Week

Topics

 

Week 1 and week 2

 

Aquaculture and Seafood Processing in BC and Canada, aquaculture organisms harvested by type and quantity. Seafood processing principles

 

·      Seafood chemistry, and natural breakdown after harvesting (microbiological route and physiologically induced)

·      Fresh and live seafood, cold chain, freezing

·      Temperature (pasteurization, cooking, smoking, HPP, canning)

·      Preserved foods (salting, curing, drying, fermentation)

·      Packaging (MAP, vacuum), regular packaging process, primary and secondary packaging.

·      Novel seafood processes: microwave sterilization, agitated retorting process, and irradiation.

 

 

Week 3 and 4

 

Seafood safety, common biological, chemical, physical, and radiological hazards. Foodborne outbreaks related to seafood products. Common pathogens of concern, preventative measures, and monitoring. Environmental monitoring programs (EMPs). Cross-contamination.

·      Listeria monocytogenes

·      Vibrio parahaemolyticus and Vibrio vulnificus

·      Clostridium botulinum, Clostridium perfringes

·      Shigella spp

·      Salmonella spp

·      E.coli

·      Norovirus, hepatitis A virus

·      Parasites (Protozoa, Diphyllobothrium spp, and Anisakis simplex)

·      Scombroid poisoning

·      Amnesic shellfish poisoning, Paralytic shellfish poisoning, Diarrheic shellfish poisoning

·      Chemical contamination (heavy metals, pesticides, hydrocarbons), and other from processing practices (sanitation chemicals, lubricants, pest control substances, hydraulics), allergens, food additives, primary packaging migration processes (evaluation of primary packaging compatibility with the food matrix and product functionality)

·      Physical contaminants, mainly foreign matter, from harvest to finished product packaging.

 

 

 

 

Week 5 and week 6

 

 

 

 

HACCP, hazard analysis, and critical control points. Risk assessment and risk management. Process controls and cGMP’s, CFIA regulations. Traceability and recall procedures.

 

Week 7 and week 8

 

 

Quality management using the HACCP model, sensory analysis, and other qualitative and quantitative tests related to quality assurance. Positive release principle.

 

 

Week 9

 

 

Salmon and trout, roe. Fresh processing, smoking, cold smoking, candied processing, frozen products. Roe brining and freezing. Food safety and quality controls, monitoring, and verification.

 

 

Week 10

 

 

Finfish management from harvest to table, freezing. EMP and farm water monitoring. Food safety and quality controls, monitoring, and verification.

 

 

Week 11

 

 

Bivalves, mussels, oysters, and clams, live oyster management from harvest to table, freezing. EMP and farm water monitoring. Food safety and quality controls, monitoring, and verification.

 

 

Week 12

 

 

Field trip. TBA.

 

Week 13

 

 

CFIA, guest speaker, seafood regulations, and licensing
