2023W: LWS 550 – Professional Communication Strategies

BACKGROUND

Effective communication skills are an essential part of becoming a successful environmental professional. Employers consistently require (and value!) strong writing and presentation skills and the ability to work collaboratively in a team. Issues related to land and water are inherently complex and involve multiple jurisdictions and stakeholders, often requiring professionals to write and speak to a variety of audiences. For example, a consultant or public servant may be asked to liaise between scientists and the public or collaborate with different stakeholders, including planners and policy makers. Learning to communicate effectively, both orally and in writing, is crucial to establishing and advancing your career in the resource management sector.

In this course, we will develop our writing and presentation skills and practise collaborating in a team environment.

RELATION TO LWS 548 – MLWS MAJOR PROJECT

LWS 550 is designed to equip MLWS students with a variety of tools and experiences to improve their communication skills in the workforce. The course will culminate in a group project where you will co- create and present together – this exercise is designed to build and enhance your team work and collaboration skills. When developing your professional communication strategy for the group project, keep in mind your goals for your LWS 548 Major Project.

This course will combine Communications theory and technical and multimedia skills, which will help to develop your professional communication strategy for the Team Project.

 

LEARNING OUTCOMES

By the end of this course, learners will have improved their writing, speaking and critical thinking skills and worked in a collaborative team environment on a class presentation related to land and water systems.

Specifically, students will be able to:

  1. Discuss ethical considerations of professionals and their communications
  2. Work effectively in a collaborative team environment
  3. Develop and deliver professional communications in a credible and rigorous manner
  4. Develop writing and summarization skills
  5. Become familiar with different formats of informal, and scientific writing formats
  6. Participate comfortably in formal and informal conversations and presentations

 

STUDENT RESPONSIBILITIES

Participation and collaboration are key features of the LWS 550 course. Assignments will include individual and group work. Students must come to class prepared, and take ownership of their roles and responsibilities in individual and group work. If you miss a class, it is your responsibility to find out what you missed and make it up on your own time. Your team project will be self and peer reviewed, and you will have an opportunity to present your views on your own and others’ contributions.

UNIVERSITY POLICIES

UBC provides resources to support student learning and to maintain healthy lifestyles but recognizes that sometimes crises arise and so there are additional resources to access including those for survivors of sexual violence. UBC values respect for the person and ideas of all members of the academic community. Harassment and discrimination are not tolerated nor is suppression of academic freedom. UBC provides appropriate accommodation for students with disabilities and for religious observances. UBC values academic honesty and students are expected to acknowledge the ideas generated by others and to uphold the highest academic standards in all of their actions.

Details of the policies and how to access support are available on the UBC Senate website.

ACADEMIC INTEGRITY

The academic enterprise is founded on honesty, civility, and integrity. As members of this enterprise, all students are expected to know, understand, and follow the codes of conduct regarding academic integrity. This means submitting only original work done by you and acknowledging all sources of information or ideas and attributing them to others as required. Plagiarism is a serious offence and may result in a mark of zero. In addition, violations of academic integrity (cheating, copying or misleading others about what is your own work) constitutes a breakdown of the academic enterprise, and will have serious consequences, potentially including referral to the President’s Advisory Committee on Student Discipline.
