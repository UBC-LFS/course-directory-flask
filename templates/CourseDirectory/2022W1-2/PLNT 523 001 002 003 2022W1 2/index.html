PLNT 523 – Seminar in Plant Science 2022/2023

 

Instructor – Dr. Risa Sargent

 

Schedule – Fridays at 3PM, Orchard Commons 4018 (first class on Sept. 9)

Grading

To pass the course each year you must:

-Attend the weekly seminar

-Participate (asking questions, joining discussions)

-Create a supportive environment (not dominating the discussion, ensuring other students have opportunities to ask questions; offering supportive, constructive critique of peer seminars)

-Present one seminar each year of your degree program

-Submit written peer feedback each week (of high quality with clear evidence of engagement in the seminar)

 

Grading/Credits

-Grading for the course is pass/fail. You must present a seminar once per year, attend the course and submit student feedback forms each week in order to receive a pass.

-Credit is attained after two years of attendance (MSc) or after four years for a PhD program.

 

Course Objectives:

  1. The ability to communicate is a core skill in science, this course is designed to allow students, in a non-competitive and supportive environment, to learn and practice this skill.
  2. Through the course, students will develop their skills in the delivery, participation and evaluation of oral scientific presentations. During the course students will be able to offer constructive criticism on presentation style and delivery and use this knowledge to improve their own seminars.
  3. Students will develop their skills in presenting scientific methods, results and interpretation; and discussing specific issues and also the successes of a variety of studies in their field and in adjacent fields related to plant science.
